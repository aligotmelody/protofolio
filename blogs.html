Open Authorization

[DATE: 2025-01-02] [AUTHOR: ALI] [TAGS: SECURITY, WEB]

Open Authorization (OAuth) is a secure, standardized framework that lets users grant third-party applications limited access to their data without sharing passwords.

Introduction

Hey there! In this post, I'll simplify how OAuth works by breaking down a custom server I built. I needed to build it to truly understand the attack vectors.

Terminology

1. The Frontchannel (Browser)

Like a public courier. Data is sent via URL redirects. Risk: Visible in history and logs. Analogy: A postcard.

2. The Backchannel (Server-to-Server)

A private secure line using HTTPS. Security: Encrypted and invisible to the user. Analogy: A private phone call.
